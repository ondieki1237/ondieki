Nelson Rolihlahla Mandela was a South African anti-apartheid activist who served as the first president of South Africa from 1994 to 1999. He was the country's first black head of state and the first elected in a fully representative democratic election.

  • He became the president of the ANC youth league in 1950
  • Nelson Mandera became the leading opponent of the 1948 anti-apartheid legislation
  • He co founded the first black legal partnership in South Africa
  • He with other leaders played a major role in ending the apartheid regine
  • Nelson became the first black African president
  • He also served as the secretary general of the Non Aligned Movement
  • Nelson won the Nobel Peace Prize 1993
